Splat

Atlas comes with powerful splatmap control. Blend and filter geographically based textures and write them to splatmap layers onto your terrain. Unity supports up to 8 splatmap layers but since your terrain solution might not be default up to 16 splatmap layers are supported trough atlas.It is also possible to utilize these layers for other purposes aswel like placing vegitation or using them for biome techniques.

Roads and Splines

Atlas would not be complete without splines, wich can be used to make rivers, coast lines and connecting road networks. The spline tool works fast and effortless, the nodes are non selection based wich means we can simply drag them around. The base shortcuts like holding down [shift] and/or [control] are used to scale / add / remove nodes on the spline. The spline stamps come with a special road blending feature wich will make it alot easyer to blend your road network. The splines can also build a mesh wich can serve as your river water and/or road mesh.

APPLY

Atlas can apply your stamps on as many terrain instances as you like.
It does this seamlessly and it can do it without terrain stitching wich means terrains are not required to be in a perfect grid and they do not require to share the same settings / resolution.

Adaptive performance

Atlas has an edit mode, wich means you can disable atlas entirly while you are not editing the terrain.
to make sure we can work on huge maps like 16/16 4K tiles even on potato computers we implemented a performance enshurance policy.You can choose the resolution you want to use while editing the terrain wich locks the total amount of pixels rendered. Making it sothat performance stays thesame no mater how big you go. While editing atlas will show you a lower resolution preview of the entire terrain and it also shows you a smaller square at full resolution.The full resolution square can be moved around and it ensures that inside the full resolution square it will look like the final result.
